Gossiping Tweens and Ending Regimes: The Promises and Pitfalls of the Doctrine of Double Effect

Karis E Morasch

Each day of our lives, we are required to weigh the good and bad of our choices and make the decision we choose as best. Oftentimes, we don't judge the morality of our actions based on any particular framework, but rather use our intuitive judgements to the best of our ability. The task of moral philosophers is to create a framework that aligns with our intuitive judgements that will allow us to retrospectively or proactively gauge the morality of a particular action. Several of these moral frameworks focus on the balance of good and bad in the result of a particular action to determine moral praiseworthy or blameworthiness of the action, a view known as consequentialism. ...
